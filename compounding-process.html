Compounding Process

The mechanics of the snowball effect.

The Multiplier Logic

Compounding occurs when you earn a return on both your initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. In mathematical terms, your multiplier is defined as 1 + r.

Growth Formula:
A = P(1 + r)ⁿ

P = Starting Amount
r = Rate of Growth
n = Number of Steps

Linear vs Exponential

Linear growth adds a fixed amount, while exponential growth scales the entire total. Over time, the gap between the two becomes massive.

Dashed: Linear | Solid: Compounding

Example: $1,000 at 5%
Step 1: $1,000 × 1.05 = $1,050
Step 2: $1,050 × 1.05 = $1,102.50
Step 10: $1,000 × 1.05¹⁰ = $1,628.89
